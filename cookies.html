1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites place on your device (computer, tablet, or phone) when you visit them. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, remember your preferences, and provide information to the website owners.

In addition to traditional cookies, modern websites may use other browser storage technologies such as localStorage and sessionStorage, which function similarly but offer different capabilities in terms of data capacity and persistence.

This policy covers all forms of browser storage used by the Xstream movies platform — not just cookies, but also localStorage and any other client-side data storage mechanisms.

2. Types of Browser Storage We Use

We keep our use of browser storage to an absolute minimum. Here are the categories we use:

Essential

Authentication Cookies

Set by Firebase Auth to maintain your logged-in session. Without these, you would need to sign in every time you visit a new page on the Platform.

Essential

localStorage Preferences

Used to store your like/dislike history and viewed video IDs locally in your browser. This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to our servers. Keys used: sv_liked, sv_disliked, sv_viewed.

Not Used

Analytics Cookies

We do not use Google Analytics, Hotjar, Mixpanel, or any other analytics service. No behavioral tracking cookies are set.

Not Used

Advertising Cookies

We do not serve advertisements on the Platform. No ad networks, retargeting pixels, or ad-related cookies are used.

Not Used

Social Media Cookies

We do not embed Facebook, Twitter, or other social media widgets that would set tracking cookies on our pages.

3. Detailed Breakdown

Below is a complete table of all browser storage items used by the Platform:

Storage Item Type Set By Purpose Duration
__session Cookie Firebase Auth Maintains login session across page requests Session (expires when browser closes)
firebaseAuthUser Cookie Firebase Auth Persists authentication state across browser sessions Up to 2 weeks (if "Remember Me" is selected)
sv_liked localStorage Xstream Stores IDs of videos you've liked Permanent until manually cleared

sv_viewed localStorage Xstream Stores IDs of videos you've viewed (prevents double-counting views) Permanent until manually cleared

That's it. Five items total. No hidden trackers, no third-party cookies, no fingerprinting scripts.

The only third-party service that may set cookies on our Platform is Google Firebase, which sets authentication cookies as described in the table above. These cookies are essential for the Platform to function.

Cloudinary may set standard CDN cookies for caching and performance optimization. These are not set by or controlled by us. Cloudinary's cookie policy applies: cloudinary.com/privacy

No other third parties set cookies through our Platform.

5. How to Manage & Delete

5.1 Clearing Xstream localStorage

To clear your like/dislike/view history stored in localStorage:

  1. Open your browser's Developer Tools (F12 or Ctrl+Shift+I / Cmd+Shift+I)
  2. Go to the "Application" or "Storage" tab
  3. Find "Local Storage" in the left sidebar
  4. Click on our domain
  5. Find and delete the keys: sv_liked, sv_disliked, sv_viewed

Or simply use your browser's "Clear browsing data" function and select "Cookies and other site data" along with "Site data".

5.2 Managing Cookies in Your Browser

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. The process varies by browser:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies

For more details, visit allaboutcookies.org/manage-cookies

5.3 Disabling Cookies Entirely

You can set your browser to block all cookies. However, this will prevent Firebase Auth from maintaining your login session, meaning you will not be able to stay logged in as you navigate the Platform. The site will still be viewable as a guest, but you will not be able to upload videos, like/dislike content, or access your profile.

6. Impact of Disabling Storage

If you block Firebase Auth cookies

You will be automatically signed out and unable to stay logged in. Each page navigation will require re-authentication. Uploading videos and accessing your profile will not function properly.

If you clear localStorage

Your like/dislike history will be reset. Videos you previously liked/disliked will show as neutral. Videos you've already viewed may have their view count incremented again if you watch them.

If you block all cookies

The Platform will function in a limited guest mode. You can browse and watch videos, but all account-related features (login, upload, like, profile) will be unavailable.

If you use private/incognito mode

The Platform works normally in private browsing. Note that localStorage data will be cleared when you close the private window, so your like/dislike history will not persist between sessions.
